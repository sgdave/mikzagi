Logging:
Say you're speaking to a company that we need to buy data from... Aka OBC
1. Figure out if they fit the bill. Do they have star trackers? How many? Are they commercial off the shelf (COTS) or do they buy parts and build it themselves? How do they calibrate it? What orbits and altitudes do they go to? Can they reprogram their star trackers? To what extent? Can they get the basic data we need out and down to ground in a timely manner? What's the latency? What duty cycle can they provide this at? Are they willing to work together on optimization techniques that work in favor of both? Answering some of these questions is possible online but most require an NDA and a discussion with that company directly to ask. Tell them to bring their star tracker expert.
2. If they pass most things, assess the business. Are they likely to succeed in doing what they do? Are they a start-up or big business? What country do they operate in or the satellites registered to? What's required legally and practically to make a deal with them.
3. What does this cost them? What is the price of their downlink currently and are they targeting to bring that down? They have a cut off downlink target, and we have a minimum required data value for financial success.
4. When is the data going to be available? Is this ready today or will it take a long time. Do we need to contribute resources? What will it cost us vs what we get and when.
5. If not OBC but data is ready or easily ready, Can they provide a sample dataset for assessment? Price and costing can be discussed in future chats.
